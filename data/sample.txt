FRAUD INVESTIGATION REPORT — Q3 2024

Account Takeover Fraud:
Account takeover attacks increased 23% in Q3 2024.
Primary attack vectors include credential stuffing using
leaked password databases, SIM swapping targeting
high-value accounts, and phishing campaigns mimicking
legitimate bank communications. Affected accounts showed
login attempts from multiple countries within 24 hours.

Synthetic Identity Fraud:
Synthetic identity fraud cases rose 15% quarter over quarter.
Fraudsters combine real Social Security numbers with fake
names and dates of birth. These synthetic identities are
built up over 6-18 months before being used for large
fraudulent transactions. Detection signals include thin
credit files with sudden large transaction requests.

Transaction Fraud Patterns:
High-risk transaction signals identified this quarter:
purchases over $500 within first 48 hours of account creation,
multiple failed payment attempts followed by success,
transactions at unusual hours (2am-5am local time),
and rapid fund transfers to newly added external accounts.

Recommended Actions:
Implement velocity checks on new accounts for first 30 days.
Flag transactions over $200 from accounts under 7 days old.
Monitor for impossible travel — logins from cities more than
500 miles apart within 2 hours. Deploy device fingerprinting
to detect emulators used in automated fraud attacks.
